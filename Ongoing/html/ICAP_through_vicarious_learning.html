This section is completely optional. However, there is a substantial body of literature that suggests that watching videos of expert-novice interactions is more effective than watching experts. This work was pioneered and originally popularized by Derek Muller (TED Video overview). It was rigorously studied and further developed by Miki Chi (Miki's paper), who found that watching videos of expert-novice interactions together in small groups, with the ability to pause, discuss, and rewind, was almost as effective as 1:1 tutoring. Results on student enjoyment have been mixed; in some cases, students find such videos confusing (but learn more!), and in some cases, they like the additional personality of these videos.
