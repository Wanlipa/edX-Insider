First of all, it is a helpful to understand this aspect of the platform. Second of all, as a deep dive, it is a good examplar of how technology can act as an enabler for key concepts from learning science. This is one of the many techniques applied within the edX platform.

Integrated Learning Sequences

A goal of the design of the edX platform was to start using the technology in more active and constructive ways. While this term is oft-debated in education circles, there is a clear delineation between people watching videos passively and being actively engaged in learning. If we make a simple comparison between, for example:

  1. A student watches a long video. 
  2. A student watches a video with questions asked every 15 minutes to check understanding. 
  3. A student watch a sequence of 1-4 minute videos interleaved with problems, simulations, and discussions where the student is actively engaged in constructing their understanding of the material. 

With each step, we are moving towards a place where the student is more engaged in the learning process, and learns more enjoyably and effectively. 

ICAP:

The theory underlying this approach is from Michelene Chi. Her work in physics education stretches back to the 1980s and the particular work we are discussing is the ICAP framework. It presents a Passive/Active/Constructive/Interactive categorization that might be useful for us as a framework for discussing the design of the platform.
